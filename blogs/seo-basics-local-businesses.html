SEO Basics for Local Businesses: How to Get Found Online

You have a beautiful website. But if no one can find it, does it exist? Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is the practice of making your website visible to people searching for your products or services. For local businesses — restaurants, plumbers, dentists, boutiques — local SEO is the key to attracting nearby customers. Here are five simple, high‑impact techniques you can implement today.

1. Claim and Optimize Your Google Business Profile

Formerly known as Google My Business, this free listing is the most important local SEO factor. When people search for "coffee shop near me," Google displays local pack results based on your profile. Make sure your name, address, phone number (NAP), hours, and categories are accurate. Add photos, respond to reviews, and post updates regularly.

2. Use Local Keywords

Include your city, neighbourhood, or region in key areas: page titles, meta descriptions, headings, and body text. For example, instead of "best bakery," use "best bakery in Connaught Place, Delhi." Think about what your customers type into Google.

3. Optimize for Mobile

Over 60% of local searches happen on mobile devices. Google uses mobile‑first indexing, meaning it looks at your mobile site first. Ensure your site is responsive, loads quickly, and has click‑to‑call buttons. Google's PageSpeed Insights can help you identify issues.

"Local SEO isn't about beating the algorithm. It's about being found by people in your community who need you."

4. Build Local Citations and Backlinks

A citation is any online mention of your business NAP. Get listed in local directories (Yelp, Justdial, local chambers of commerce). Earn backlinks from local news sites, blogs, or suppliers. Quality matters more than quantity.

5. Use Schema Markup

Schema.org markup helps search engines understand your content. Add LocalBusiness schema to your website. It can display star ratings, operating hours, and location directly in search results — increasing click‑through rates.

Conclusion
